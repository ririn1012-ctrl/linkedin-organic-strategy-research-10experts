LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

A Practitioner Research Repository


Goal: Build a research base strong enough to support a real content playbook for B2B SaaS companies using LinkedIn organic as a primary growth channel.




What This Is

This repo captures original LinkedIn posts, frameworks, and content patterns from 10 practitioners who actively do the thing they teach — founders who built companies using content, operators with real revenue attached to their strategies, not consultants who've drifted into thought leadership.

The research is designed to answer:


What content formats are actually working for B2B SaaS on LinkedIn in 2024–2025?
What patterns distinguish high-engagement posts from noise?
What are the underlying strategic frameworks these practitioners share?



Expert Selection Criteria

We rejected:


"LinkedIn coaches" with no operating background
Marketers who talk about content but show no company results
Anyone whose primary product is their LinkedIn following (e.g., Neil Patel, Gary V)


We selected for:


Built or scaled a real SaaS product / B2B business
Posts with demonstrably high engagement relative to audience size
Content that includes frameworks, not just takes
Active posting in 2024–2025

Key Research Findings (From /other/cross-expert-patterns.md)

What All 10 Share


Posts from experience, not theory — every credibility anchor is operational, not academic
Take a clear position — no hedging; "per-seat pricing is dying," not "there are various pricing models worth considering"
Close with a portable principle — the last line is always the most quotable; it's what gets screenshotted
Never promote while teaching — educational content and CTA content are strictly separated
Serve the reader's problem, not their own message — starts from pain, not from product

Format Performance

Carousels (PDFs) consistently show the highest engagement rate (~6.6%) among all LinkedIn formats in 2024–2025, driven by "dwell time" mechanics. Most experts rotate between short text posts (daily/high-frequency) and carousels (weekly/high-effort).
