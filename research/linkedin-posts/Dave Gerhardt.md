## Post 3 - "Here's what I'd do in my first 90 days as CMO of a B2B SaaS company"

**Date:** ~Q2 2024

**Format:** Text + short list

**Engagement:** ~4,800 reactions, 520 comments

### Post

> Here's what I'd do in my first 90 days as CMO of a B2B SaaS company:
>
> ### Month 1: Listen
>
> Talk to 20 customers. Not to sell. To understand.
>
> Talk to 10 sales reps. What do they hear most?
>
> Read every churned customer note you can find.
>
> Don't change anything yet.
>
> ### Month 2: Clarify
>
> What's the one thing this company is best at?
>
> What's the one type of customer who loves it most?
>
> Rewrite the homepage headline (you'll probably hate what's there).
>
> Kill the content that exists just to fill a calendar.
>
> ### Month 3: Build
>
> Launch one content format and do it consistently.
>
> Set up the attribution system (even a simple one).
>
> Find the one channel where your ICP spends time.
>
> Start the community or newsletter now (it takes a year to matter).
>
> Most CMOs sprint to launch campaigns.
>
> The best ones sprint to understand first.

### Why this post works

Extremely high-utility post - gives a complete framework in under 200 words. The month-by-month structure is easy to share with a team. Gets bookmarked. Positions listening before building as counterintuitive wisdom.

**Pattern:**
`Numbered timeline → action bullets per phase → surprise ending reframe`

## Post 5 - "Exit Five crossed 10,000 members this week"

**Date:** ~Q1 2025

**Format:** Milestone post with lesson

**Engagement:** ~5,200 reactions, 610 comments

### Post

> Exit Five crossed 10,000 members this week.
>
> A few things I know to be true about building a B2B community:
>
> The niche is the point. "B2B marketers" is specific enough to resonate, broad enough to scale. Most communities are too vague ("marketing professionals") or too narrow ("demand gen managers at Series B SaaS").
>
> You need a place. We have a private Slack. People don't just follow content - they come back for the conversations.
>
> Email is the engine. LinkedIn built the awareness. Email built the retention. I send a newsletter every week and it drives more paid membership than any ad ever has.
>
> Charge for something. Paid members show up differently. Free-only communities become ghost towns.
>
> The founder is the product. For 2 years, I answered every Slack message, replied to every email, showed up in every call. You can't outsource the early stage of community.

### Why this post works

Milestone posts with transparent lessons are consistently Gerhardt's highest-performing content. He doesn't just celebrate - he teaches. Five numbered learnings are easy to repost and debate.

**Pattern:**
`Milestone anchor → number of learnings → each lesson is 2 sentences (statement + why it matters)`
