# LinkedIn Algorithm Research (2024–2025)

> Supporting material for B2B SaaS content strategy research  
> Sources: Richard van der Blom's 2025 Algorithm Report, LinkedIn Marketing Labs data, Averi.ai B2B SaaS LinkedIn guide, independent creator tracking

---

## Overview

LinkedIn's algorithm has shifted significantly since 2023. Understanding these mechanics is critical for any B2B SaaS content strategy — they explain why certain expert post formats (carousels, no-link posts, personal accounts over brand pages) consistently outperform.

---

## Key Algorithm Findings (2024–2025)

### Organic Reach Has Declined Significantly
- Organic views per post down ~50% compared to prior year (van der Blom, 2025)
- BUT: engagement per post is up ~12%
- Interpretation: smaller audiences are engaging more deeply; quantity of reach no longer the primary success metric

### Overall Engagement Rates Are Rising
- Platform average engagement rate: 3.85% (up 44% YoY as of mid-2025)
- Engagement by impressions: ~5.20%
- Source: Averi.ai B2B LinkedIn analysis, Dec 2025

### Format Performance Hierarchy (2024–2025 Data)

| Format | Average Engagement Rate | Notes |
|--------|------------------------|-------|
| Carousels (PDF uploads) | 6.60% | Highest of any format; swipe mechanic creates dwell time |
| Video (native uploads) | 5.50% | Must be uploaded directly, not YouTube links |
| Text-only posts | 3.80% | Performs best when hook is strong; no-link posts get more reach |
| Image posts | 3.40% | Declining; algorithm deprioritizing static images |
| External link posts | 1.20% | LinkedIn actively suppresses posts with outbound links |
| Polls | 4.90% | High engagement but low signal-to-noise ratio |

### Dwell Time as Primary Signal
- LinkedIn's algorithm uses "dwell time" (how long someone pauses on a post) as a primary quality signal
- This explains Justin Welsh's consistent advice to use line breaks (forces users to scroll through the post, increasing dwell time)
- Long posts with strong structure perform better than short posts with all content in the preview

### Personal Profiles vs. Brand Pages
- Personal profiles get 3–5x more organic reach than company/brand pages
- This is the core mechanic behind "employee advocacy" and founder content strategies
- Most high-performing B2B SaaS companies (per Chris Walker's research) have 3–5 personal LinkedIn accounts posting, not a brand page

### The "Golden Hour" Effect
- First 60–90 minutes of engagement after posting heavily influences reach
- Posts that get comments (not just likes) in the first hour see ~3x more distribution
- This is why many creators ask a question or post a contrarian take — it baits early comments

### Commenting Strategy
- Thoughtful comments under others' posts (especially from high-follower accounts) can drive significant profile visits
- Many of the experts in this research are as visible in comment threads as in original posts

## The "Dark Social" Overlap

LinkedIn analytics only capture a fraction of real engagement. Key dark social behaviors that don't show in data:

- **DMs**: "Hey, I saw your post about X — can we talk?" never appears in post analytics
- **Screenshots to Slack/Teams**: Highly shareable content often travels through internal company channels with zero attribution
- **Email forwards**: "Saw this, thought of you" — the post reached a new person but LinkedIn doesn't know

Chris Walker estimates that self-reported attribution ("How did you first hear about us?") catches 30–50% more meaningful touchpoints than analytics tools.

## Optimal Post Structure (2025)

Based on algorithm mechanics and pattern analysis of top-performing posts:

### Line 1 (The Hook)
- Must stand alone as worth clicking "see more"
- Best performing hooks: counterintuitive claim, specific number, question that creates tension
- Avoid: "I'm excited to share...", "Quick tip:", "A thought..."

### Lines 2–7 (The Body)
- Line breaks after every 1–2 sentences
- Each line should be worth reading alone
- No bullet lists without context — each bullet should be a complete thought

### Final Line (The Closer)
- Almost all high-performing posts end with a standalone principle
- Often the most quotable/shareable line in the post
- Avoid question-bait at the end unless the question is genuinely interesting

### Links
- If you must include a link, put it in comments
- LinkedIn penalizes posts with outbound links in the body
- "Link in comments" is algorithmically correct and widely accepted

## What's Changed vs. 2022–2023

| Tactic | 2022–2023 | 2024–2025 |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Daily posting | Required for growth | Less critical; 3x/week optimal |
| Hashtags | Important for discovery | Largely algorithmic noise |
| Polls | High reach | Declining; algorithm deprioritizing |
| Long articles | Native reach | Better to post excerpts + link |
| Company page posting | Viable reach | Heavily deprioritized |
| "Comment to get X" | High engagement hack | Now penalized |
| No-link text posts | Good reach | Still best for reach |
| PDF carousels | Moderate | Now highest-performing format |
